    1Introduction

    Welcome to the Privacy Policy for XPlayer Pro, an Android-based video player application designed to deliver a high-quality local media playback experience. This policy describes in detail how we handle information in connection with your use of the application.

    XPlayer Pro is built with a privacy-first philosophy. Unlike many applications that collect extensive personal data, our video player operates primarily on your device. The majority of your media files, playlists, settings, and viewing history never leave your phone or tablet.

    We collect only the minimum information necessary to operate the application, deliver relevant advertisements, understand usage patterns through analytics, process in-app purchases, and ensure application stability through crash reporting. Every piece of data we do collect serves a specific, documented purpose described in this policy.

    Key Principle: XPlayer Pro does not upload, transmit, or share your personal video files, photos, or media library contents with us or any third party. All media processing happens locally on your device.

    By installing and using XPlayer Pro, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with any part of this policy, you should uninstall the application and discontinue use.

    This policy applies exclusively to XPlayer Pro and does not govern the practices of third-party websites, services, or applications that you may access through links or advertisements displayed within the application.

    8Diagnostics

    XPlayer Pro collects diagnostic information to monitor application health and performance. This data helps us identify performance bottlenecks, memory leaks, and other technical issues before they affect a large number of users.

    • Memory Usage: Peak and average RAM consumption during a session.
    • CPU Utilization: Processor load during video decoding and rendering operations.
    • Battery Impact: Estimated battery consumption attributed to the application.
    • Frame Rate Metrics: Video rendering frame rates and instances of frame drops during playback.
    • Startup Time: How long the application takes to initialize and become interactive.
    • ANR Events: Application Not Responding events, including the duration and the activity where they occurred.

    This diagnostic data is collected and transmitted by Firebase Crashlytics and Firebase Analytics. It is associated with your Advertising ID or an anonymous installation identifier, not with your name, email, or any personal account.

    9Crash Reports

    When XPlayer Pro encounters an unexpected error and terminates abruptly, a detailed crash report is generated and transmitted to Firebase Crashlytics. These reports are essential for identifying and fixing bugs.

    • Stack Trace: The complete sequence of method calls leading to the crash, including file names and line numbers.
    • Exception Type: The classification of the error (e.g., NullPointerException, OutOfMemoryError, IllegalStateException).
    • Thread State: Which thread the crash occurred on and the state of other active threads.
    • Device Context: Device model, Android version, available memory at the time of crash, and whether the device was rooted.
    • App State: Which screen or activity was active, whether background play was running, and the app version.
    • Custom Logs: Limited diagnostic logs that we explicitly mark for inclusion in crash reports. These logs do not contain personal content or file names.
    Warning: Crash reports may contain fragmentary references to file paths on your device. These are structural path references from the stack trace, not deliberate collection of your file names. We do not use these path fragments for any purpose beyond debugging.

    10Media Files

    This section addresses one of the most important aspects of XPlayer Pro's privacy posture: how your media files are handled.

    Local Processing Only

    XPlayer Pro processes all media files entirely on your device. When you play a video, the application reads the file from your device's storage, decodes it using the device's hardware or software codecs, and renders it on your screen. At no point during this process is the file content uploaded, streamed, or transmitted to any server.

    What XPlayer Pro Does With Your Files

    • Reads video and audio files from storage for playback.
    • Reads subtitle files (SRT, ASS, SSA, VTT) for overlay during playback.
    • Scans your storage directories to build an in-app media library index.
    • Stores playback positions (bookmark timestamps) locally so you can resume where you left off.
    • Generates thumbnail images from video files, stored locally in the application's cache directory.
    • Caches recently played file metadata locally for faster library loading.

    What XPlayer Pro Does NOT Do

    • Does not upload video files to any server.
    • Does not transmit file names, folder structures, or media metadata to our servers.
    • Does not share your media library contents with advertisers or analytics providers.
    • Does not scan file contents for any purpose other than media playback.
    • Does not modify your original media files without your explicit action.
    Your media library is yours alone. The file names in your video folder, the content of your videos, and your personal photos are never transmitted externally by XPlayer Pro.

    11Local Storage Access

    XPlayer Pro accesses your device's local storage to locate and play media files. The nature and scope of this access depends on your Android version and the permissions you have granted.

    Android VersionAccess MethodScope
    Android 12 and belowREAD_EXTERNAL_STORAGEBroad access to shared storage
    Android 13 and aboveREAD_MEDIA_VIDEO, READ_MEDIA_IMAGESScoped to specific media types
    Android 14 and aboveREAD_MEDIA_VIDEO + MANAGE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE (optional)Granular media access; full access only if explicitly granted

    All data read from local storage is processed on-device. XPlayer Pro creates its own application-private directories for:

    • Internal Cache: Thumbnail images, decoded subtitle caches, and temporary playback buffers. Automatically managed by Android.
    • Internal Files: Application configuration, playback position database, playlist data, and private folder encryption metadata.
    • External App Directory: Exported playlists, downloaded subtitle files, and extracted audio files stored in a dedicated XPlayer Pro folder.

    12Photos and Videos Permission

    On Android 13 (API 33) and above, XPlayer Pro requests the READ_MEDIA_VIDEO and READ_MEDIA_IMAGES permissions to access your media files. These are the modern, privacy-focused replacements for the legacy READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE permission.

    • READ_MEDIA_VIDEO: Required to scan your device for video files, display them in the media library, and play them back. Without this permission, XPlayer Pro cannot locate or play any video content.
    • READ_MEDIA_IMAGES: Requested to support thumbnail generation from video files and to enable any image-related features within the application.

    The permission request appears when you first launch XPlayer Pro and attempt to access the video library. If you deny the permission, the application will function in a limited mode.

    User Control: You can revoke this permission at any time through your Android device Settings > Apps > XPlayer Pro > Permissions. Revoking the permission will prevent the app from accessing new media files but will not delete any files from your device.

    13Notification Permission

    On Android 13 (API 33) and above, XPlayer Pro requests the POST_NOTIFICATIONS permission. This permission is needed for the following specific purpose:

    • Background Playback Notification: When you use the background play feature, Android requires a persistent notification to indicate that a foreground service is running. This notification displays playback controls (play, pause, next, previous) and track information.

    If you deny the notification permission, the background play feature will not function properly on Android 13 and above. Android's operating system requirements mandate a visible notification for any foreground service.

    Note: XPlayer Pro does not send promotional push notifications, marketing messages, or any unsolicited notifications. The notification permission is used exclusively for media playback controls during background play.

    14Foreground Service

    XPlayer Pro uses an Android Foreground Service with the type mediaPlayback to enable continuous audio and video playback when the application is not in the foreground.

    • When you start playing media and switch to another application, XPlayer Pro promotes the playback service to a foreground service.
    • A persistent notification appears in your notification shade with playback controls.
    • The service continues decoding and playing media until you explicitly stop playback or close the application.

    The foreground service itself does not collect or transmit any additional data. It uses the same on-device decoding pipeline as foreground playback. On Android 14 and above, the foreground service requires the FOREGROUND_SERVICE_MEDIA_PLAYBACK permission, which is declared in the application manifest and automatically granted by the system upon installation.

    21Cookies and Similar Technologies

    While cookies are primarily a web browser technology, the concept extends to mobile applications through analogous tracking and identification mechanisms.

    • Advertising ID: Functions similarly to a cookie by providing a persistent, resettable identifier for ad tracking across applications. Managed at the operating system level.
    • Firebase Installation ID (FID): A unique identifier for each app installation instance, used by Firebase services. Persists until the app is uninstalled or the FID is explicitly reset.
    • SharedPreferences: Android's local key-value storage mechanism, used to store your app preferences, settings, and state. This data never leaves your device.
    • Room Database: A local SQLite database used to store your playback history, playlists, and media library index. Entirely on-device.
    • Device Fingerprinting (Not Used): XPlayer Pro does not engage in device fingerprinting.
    WebViews: If XPlayer Pro displays any web-based content through an embedded WebView, standard web cookies may be set by the loaded website. These are governed by the website's own cookie policy.

    22Advertising ID

    The Google Advertising ID is a unique, user-resettable identifier assigned to each Android device by Google Play Services. It serves as the primary mechanism for ad personalization and measurement in the Android ecosystem.

    How XPlayer Pro Uses the Advertising ID

    • AdMob: The Advertising ID is passed to Google AdMob to serve relevant advertisements and measure ad effectiveness.
    • Firebase Analytics: The Advertising ID is used as a pseudonymous identifier to link analytics events across sessions.
    • Firebase Crashlytics: The Advertising ID may be associated with crash reports to help identify whether a specific crash pattern affects particular user segments.

    Your Control

    • Reset: Settings > Google > Ads > Reset advertising ID. This generates a new ID, severing the connection to any previous activity.
    • Opt Out: Settings > Google > Ads > Delete advertising ID. This removes the Advertising ID entirely, causing all ad requests to be treated as non-personalized.
    Privacy Advantage: Unlike persistent device identifiers (such as IMEI), the Advertising ID is designed with user privacy in mind. It can be reset at any time, and opting out is a straightforward process built into Android settings.

    29Security Measures

    We implement reasonable technical and organizational security measures to protect the limited data that XPlayer Pro collects.

    Application-Level Security

    • Encrypted Local Storage: The Private Folder feature uses Android's EncryptedFile API. The encryption key is derived from your PIN/pattern using Android's KeyStore system.
    • Secure SharedPreferences: Sensitive settings are stored using EncryptedSharedPreferences, which encrypts both keys and values.
    • No Sensitive Data in Logs: We do not log file names, file paths, user credentials, or personal data to logcat or any logging system.
    • Network Security Configuration: XPlayer Pro uses Android's Network Security Configuration to enforce HTTPS for all network communications.
    • ProGuard/R8 Obfuscation: The release build is obfuscated using R8, making reverse engineering more difficult.

    Infrastructure Security

    • Firebase project access is restricted to authorized developer accounts with two-factor authentication enabled.
    • AdMob and billing configurations are protected by Google's own security infrastructure.
    • API keys and configuration secrets are stored in non-accessible resources.
    Limitation: The security of your device itself is outside our control. We recommend keeping your Android system updated, using a screen lock, and installing applications only from trusted sources.

    45Right To Withdraw Consent

    Where our processing of your personal data is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing that occurred before the withdrawal.

    Consent-Based Processing in XPlayer Pro

    The primary consent-based processing in XPlayer Pro relates to personalized advertising through Google AdMob. Consent for personalized ads is managed through the Android Advertising ID system using an opt-out model:

    • To withdraw consent for personalized ads: Go to Settings > Google > Ads > Delete advertising ID, or opt out of ad personalization. This effectively withdraws consent for personalized advertising.
    • Effect of withdrawal: You will continue to see non-personalized ads in XPlayer Pro. The application's core video playback functionality is not affected.

    Other Withdrawal Mechanisms

    • Crash Reporting: Disable in XPlayer Pro Settings > Privacy > Crash Reporting.
    • Analytics: You can limit analytics by resetting your Advertising ID, which breaks the connection between your current sessions and historical data.
